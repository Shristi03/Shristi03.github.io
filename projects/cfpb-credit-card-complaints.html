Project Overview

This project analyzes hundreds of thousands of U.S. consumer credit card complaints sourced from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) to uncover systemic risk drivers, operational inefficiencies, and consumer harm patterns across products, geographies, and issue types.
The objective was not just descriptive analytics, but to translate complaint data into financial, operational, and regulatory insights that matter to banks, risk teams, and executives.

Dataset

  • Source: CFPB Public Consumer Complaint Database
  • Scope: Credit card–related complaints across multiple years
  • Key fields: Primary issue, Timely response flag, Resolution outcome, State, Complaint volume over time
This dataset represents real consumer friction points that directly influence cost-to-serve, regulatory scrutiny, and brand trust.

Analytical Approach

  • Trend analysis to identify long-term growth and structural shifts in complaints
  • Pareto analysis to isolate the small set of issues driving the majority of complaints
  • State-level analysis to surface geographic concentration and regulatory exposure
  • Operational risk analysis using untimely response rates as a proxy for process complexity
  • Outcome analysis to understand how complaints are resolved and at what cost
All metrics were modeled using Power BI with custom DAX measures, enabling dynamic filtering and drill-downs.

Key Findings & Business Interpretation

  • 1. Complaint Growth Signals Rising Consumer and Operational Pressure
    Total complaints increase sharply over time, particularly post-2022.
    Implication: Rising transaction volume, digital usage, and consumer sensitivity increase pressure on servicing operations and complaint management infrastructure.
  • 2. A Small Number of Issues Drive the Majority of Risk
    ~20% of issue categories generate ~80% of complaints, led by purchase disputes, acquisition issues, and credit reporting inaccuracies.
    Implication: Targeted fixes in a few high-impact areas can materially reduce complaint volume, regulatory exposure, and servicing cost.
  • 3. Acquisition and Onboarding Are Major Value Leaks
    “Getting a credit card” ranks among the top complaint drivers.
    Implication: Banks incur marketing, underwriting, and onboarding costs for customers who never successfully onboard — a customer acquisition efficiency risk with direct P&L impact.
  • 4. Untimely Responses Signal Operational Complexity
    Certain issues show disproportionately high untimely response rates (e.g., card usage, payment processing).
    Implication: These cases require cross-team coordination, lead to repeat contacts/escalations, and increase reputational risk.
  • 5. Geographic Concentration Creates Regulatory Hotspots
    Complaints are heavily concentrated in large states such as CA, TX, FL, and NY.
    Implication: These regions represent outsized regulatory and legal exposure, especially where consumer protection enforcement is strongest.

Risk & Consumer Impact Lens

Risk Type How It Manifests
Operational Risk High complaint volume, untimely responses, process breakdowns
Conduct Risk Billing disputes, inaccurate reporting, unclear disclosures
Financial Risk Increased servicing costs, refunds, remediation
Reputational Risk Consumer distrust, regulatory attention, negative sentiment
Regulatory Risk CFPB scrutiny, enforcement actions, compliance obligations

Example Metrics & DAX Logic 

Total Complaints =
COUNT(Complaint[Complaint ID])

Untimely Response Rate =
DIVIDE([Untimely Complaints], [Total Complaints])

Pareto Cumulative % =
DIVIDE([Running Total Complaints], [Total Complaints])
These measures enabled risk prioritization, not just reporting.
